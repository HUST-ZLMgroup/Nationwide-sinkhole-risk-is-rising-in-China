# Figure Title and Manuscript Text for the National GWR Training Figure

## Figure Caption

Figure 2. Predictive performance, calibration and susceptibility stratification of the national sinkhole occurrence model. a. Threshold sensitivity analysis after robust sigmoid calibration of raw GWR scores, showing validation sensitivity, specificity and F1-score across candidate probability cutoffs, with the operating cutoff fixed at 0.500. b. Calibrated probability distribution of 25,232 labelled historical training samples from the positive-negative balanced dataset, partitioned into five susceptibility classes using Jenks natural breaks. c. Statistical distributions of key performance metrics for training and test datasets, derived from repeated stratified five-fold cross-validation with ten repeats. d. Receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve, displayed as the fold-level ensemble with a mean curve and uncertainty band. e. Model reliability curve comparing predicted probabilities with empirical event frequencies, with the Brier score summarizing probabilistic error.

## Manuscript Text

The calibrated national GWR model provides a robust and probabilistically interpretable prediction framework for sinkhole occurrence across China. The model was trained using 25,232 balanced historical samples, including 12,628 collapse and 12,604 non-collapse observations, and converted raw GWR regression scores into probabilities through robust sigmoid calibration. The calibrated probabilities spanned 0.072-0.850 and showed a strongly separated training distribution, with 35.9% of samples assigned to the lowest susceptibility class and 34.8% to the highest class after Jenks natural-break stratification. This distribution indicates that the model preserves clear contrast between stable and collapse-prone settings while retaining intermediate classes for spatial susceptibility mapping. Threshold diagnostics further showed balanced classification behaviour at the operating cutoff of 0.500, where the validation subset achieved sensitivity of 0.935, specificity of 0.926, accuracy of 0.930 and F1-score of 0.931. Repeated stratified five-fold validation confirmed that model skill was stable across data partitions, with test accuracy of 0.908 ± 0.004, balanced accuracy of 0.908 ± 0.004, F1-score of 0.910 ± 0.003, ROC-AUC of 0.968 ± 0.002 and average precision of 0.962 ± 0.003. The probability estimates were also well calibrated, as reflected by a low Brier score of 0.068 ± 0.002 and a reliability curve close to the one-to-one reference. By combining discrimination, calibration and probability stratification in the same validation framework, the model avoids relying on a single accuracy statistic and provides a defensible basis for spatial risk inference. These results support using the calibrated GWR model as the probability engine for national sinkhole susceptibility mapping and future scenario projection.
